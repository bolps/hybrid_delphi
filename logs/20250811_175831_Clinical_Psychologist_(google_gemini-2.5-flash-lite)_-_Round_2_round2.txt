### **Considerations Regarding "Emotional Volatility/Mood Swings":**

The moderator's note on the item "Ha forti oscillazioni d'umore, il minuto prima si arrabbia e mi urla contro e quello successivo è dolce e si scusa" is insightful. **Suggestion for Refinement:** While this behavior *is* a form of emotional abuse and certainly contributes to the victim's distress and instability, its *primary characteristic* is the rapid and unpredictable shift in emotional state and behavior. In some clinical contexts, particularly when assessing personality or relational patterns, this *dysregulation* itself can be a core feature, rather than solely a tool of abuse. It can manifest as a lack of emotional containment, which then fuels other problematic behaviors.

Therefore, I propose that this item could be considered either:
1.  **As a primary indicator of Emotional Dysregulation (a distinct dimension)**, which then *underpins* or *co-occurs with* other abusive behaviors.
2.  **As a specific manifestation within Emotional and Psychological Abuse**, emphasizing the impact of these shifts on the victim's experience (e.g., the "walking on eggshells" phenomenon).

For this revision, and to align with the moderator's synthesis which places it under Dimension 2, I will retain it there. However, I believe it's important to acknowledge its potential to represent a more fundamental pattern of affective instability.
