**How the dimensions were derived**

* **Physical Violence** is isolated because it is the only category that mentions overt bodily harm.  
* **Emotional/Psychological Abuse** groups statements that convey devaluation, humiliation, guilt‑induction, and a climate of fear without explicit physical or financial actions.  
* **Gaslighting** captures denial of reality, threats of secret‑revelation, and unfounded accusations—behaviours that distort the victim’s perception of what actually happened.  
* **Threats & Intimidation** gathers explicit threats of harm, property damage, or self‑harm used to coerce.  
* **Controlling/Coercive Monitoring** clusters the partner’s surveillance, demands for proof of fidelity, and restrictions on time‑use—core features of coercive control.  
* **Isolation** groups attempts to cut the victim off from family/friends and disparagement of those contacts.  
* **Financial Abuse** isolates economic domination (blocking account access, controlling spending, sabotaging employment).  
* **Sexual Coercion** brings together pressure for sex and sexual blackmail via intimate images.  
* **Reputation/Image Abuse** captures public humiliation, defamation, and non‑consensual sharing of private material, which attacks the victim’s social standing.

These nine latent dimensions map closely onto empirically supported constructs in the literature on intimate‑partner violence and relational abuse (e.g., the “Power and Control Wheel,” DSM‑5 criteria for Intimate‑Partner Violence, and the ICD‑11 categories of “Abuse of a partner”). Each item was placed in the single dimension that best captures its primary abusive content, avoiding overlap while preserving conceptual clarity.
