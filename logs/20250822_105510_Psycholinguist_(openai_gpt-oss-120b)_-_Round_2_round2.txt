## Rationale for keeping **eight** dimensions  

* **Empirical consensus** – at least four of the ten expert analyses identified each of the eight themes (emotional/verbal abuse, coercive control, financial abuse, physical violence, sexual coercion, threats, fear/hyper‑vigilance, emotional neglect).  
* **Theoretical coherence** – the eight factors map onto well‑established frameworks of intimate‑partner violence (e.g., Stark’s coercive‑control model, the Duluth Power‑and‑Control Wheel) while preserving the nuance of distinct abuse mechanisms (e.g., threat‑based intimidation vs. physical assault).  
* **Psychometric parsimony** – each factor groups items that load together in the majority of factor‑analytic solutions and shows low cross‑loading, reducing measurement error and improving interpretability for researchers and clinicians.

### Take‑away for practice  

* **Scoring** – sum the items within each factor; the eight scores can be used separately or combined into a global “overall abuse” index.  
* **Intervention mapping** – each dimension points to a specific therapeutic target (e.g., “Fear & Hyper‑vigilance” → trauma‑focused anxiety work; “Emotional Neglect” → attachment‑based interventions).  

The revised eight‑factor solution therefore honors the collective expertise of the Delphi panel while refining the original model for greater conceptual clarity and practical utility.
