## What changed and why?

| **Change** | **Original structure** | **New structure** | **Rationale for the revision** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Merged Economic Abuse into Coercive Control** | Separate Dimension III (Economic Abuse) | Economic‑control items now belong to Dimension 2 (Coercive Control & Isolation) | Economic control is a *means* of maintaining overall domination; virtually every expert panel (including the Delphi synthesis) treated it as a facet of coercive control rather than a distinct domain. Keeping it separate would over‑fragment the model and create artificial overlap with the isolation items. |
| **Separated Fear/Hyper‑vigilance from Emotional Abuse** | Fear‑related items were embedded in Dimension IV (Emotional/Psychological Abuse) | New Dimension 6 isolates the victim’s internal experience of chronic anxiety and self‑censorship | The Delphi synthesis highlighted that the *subjective* feeling of being constantly on edge is conceptually distinct from the *behavioural* tactics (insults, gas‑lighting). Splitting them improves discriminant validity and aligns the factor structure with how survivors themselves often differentiate “what he does” from “how I feel living with it.” |
| **Added External Concern & Validation** | No dedicated dimension (items were scattered in Emotional Abuse) | New Dimension 7 groups the two items that refer to others’ worries | These statements do not describe the partner’s behaviour nor the victim’s internal state; they capture *social corroboration* of the abuse. Treating them as a separate factor acknowledges the importance of third‑party recognition in both assessment and intervention. |
| **Retained Healthy Relationship as a distinct positive factor** | Dimension I (Healthy / Supportive Relationship) | Unchanged (now Dimension 1) | The synthesis confirmed that a positive relational factor is useful both as a contrast to the abusive dimensions and as a protective factor in measurement models. |
| **Kept Physical/Sexual Violence and Threats/Intimidation as stand‑alone factors** | Dimensions V and VI respectively | Unchanged (now Dimensions 4 and 5) | These are empirically and theoretically distinct forms of abuse that consistently as separate factors in the literature. |
| **Minor wording adjustments** | None (original Italian statements kept verbatim) | None (exact original wording preserved) | The instruction explicitly forbade altering item text; only the grouping changed. |

## Final remarks

The seven‑factor solution respects the **empirical convergence** of the Delphi panel while preserving the **theoretical integrity** of each construct:

1. **Healthy / Supportive Relationship** – a benchmark of positive relational health.  
2. **Coercive Control & Isolation** – the umbrella that captures surveillance, social restriction, and financial domination.  
3. **Emotional Abuse & Manipulation** – verbal and psychological tactics that erode self‑worth.  
4. **Physical & Sexual Violence** – overt bodily and sexual aggression.  
5. **Threats, Intimidation & Blackmail** – fear‑based coercion without direct physical harm.  
6. **Fear & Hyper‑vigilance** – the chronic internal state of dread experienced by the survivor.  
7. **External Concern & Validation** – third‑party observations that confirm the abusive pattern.

This configuration offers a **comprehensive yet parsimonious** latent‑structure model for the 48 items, ready for confirmatory factor analysis, scale development, or clinical screening in Italian‑speaking populations.
