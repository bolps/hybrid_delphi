### **1. Coercive Control and Surveillance**
*(Aligned with Delphi synthesis, with minor refinements for linguistic cohesion)*
**Items:**
- *Mi sento controllata, ho la sensazione che non si fidi di me e che voglia sapere sempre dove sono e con chi, me lo chiede continuamente*
- *Cerca di controllare ciò che faccio e con chi mi vedo*
- *Sento che mi manda troppi messaggi o mi chiama in modo insistente*
- *Pretende prove d'amore e dimostrazioni di fedeltà nei suoi confronti*
- *Mi accusa di flirtare o di tradirlo*

**Rationale for Adjustments:**
- **Removed financial control items** (*"Controlla come spendo i miei soldi," "Mi viene chiesto di giustificare ogni acquisto"*) to **avoid overlap with Economic Abuse** (Dimension 8). While financial monitoring is a form of control, its **lexical field (money, justification) is distinct** from social surveillance (tracking location, accusations of infidelity).
- **Retained emotional control items** (e.g., *pretende prove d'amore*) because they fit the **semantic field of coercive demands for loyalty**, a hallmark of surveillance-based control (Stark, 2007).

**Key Theme:**
*Systematic monitoring of behavior, social interactions, and emotional loyalty to enforce dominance.*

### **2. Emotional Abuse and Devaluation**
*(Aligned with Delphi, with expanded rationale for linguistic patterns)*
**Items:**
- *Mi butta giù di morale, mi insulta o mi critica*
- *Mi fa sentire spesso sbagliata e mi dice che è colpa mia se sta male*
- *Mi dice che non valgo niente e che resterei sola se non stessi con lui perché nessuno mi vorrebbe*
- *Urla o mi umilia di fronte ad altre persone*
- *Non mi fa sentire bene quando parla di come mi prendo cura di me stessa e del mio aspetto fisico*
- *Mi sento giudicata rispetto alle cose che faccio e a come organizzo la mia vita*

**Rationale for Adjustments:**
- **No changes to items**, but **emphasized the linguistic structure**:
  - **Declarative statements of inadequacy** (*"non valgo niente," "resterei sola"*) → **global self-worth attacks**.
  - **Conditional threats** (*"se non stessi con lui"*) → **binding the victim’s value to the relationship**.
  - **Public/private humiliation** (*"urla... di fronte ad altre persone," "mi critica"*) → **social vs. intimate shaming**.
- **Distinct from Gaslighting** (Dimension 6): While both involve manipulation, this dimension focuses on **explicit devaluation**, whereas gaslighting distorts reality.

**Key Theme:**
*Verbal/emotional attacks designed to erode self-esteem and create dependency.*

**Rationale for Adjustments:**

- **Retained *"non mi fa sentire libera di vestirmi"*** as it reflects **autonomy over personal expression**, a subtle but critical form of isolation (e.g., dictating appearance to align with abuser’s preferences).

**Key Theme:**
*Limiting social support and personal freedom to increase dependence.*

**Key Theme:**
*Inducing fear through explicit or enacted harm.*

*(Expanded to include emotional unpredictability; renamed for clarity)*

- **Added mood swings** because they **create an unstable environment**, undermining the victim’s sense of **predictability and safety** (a hallmark of traumatic bonding).
- **Distinct from Gaslighting**: This dimension focuses on **emotional climate**, not **reality distortion**.

**Key Theme:**
*Absence of safety, support, and consistency in the relationship.*
