### Rationale for the final eight‑factor model  

1. **Parsimony with theoretical fidelity** – The eight factors cover all seven consensus themes (emotional abuse, coercive control, threats/intimidation, physical/sexual violence, isolation, fear, gas‑lighting) plus a reverse‑coded healthy‑relationship item for validity.  
2. **Respect for major points of disagreement** –  
   * Gas‑lighting is kept separate (Dimension 3) because a sizable minority of experts argued for its distinctiveness.  
   * Financial abuse is subsumed under coercive control (Dimension 1) following the majority view that money‑restriction is a control tool.  
   * Jealousy/possessiveness, mood‑swings and emotional neglect are incorporated into broader clusters where they most naturally fit.  
3. **Non‑overlapping assignment** – Each statement appears in **exactly one** factor, satisfying the requirement for a clean factor‑analytic solution.  
4. **Clinical relevance** – The dimensions map cleanly onto established IPV frameworks (e.g., the Power‑and‑Control Wheel, DSM‑5‑TR/ICD‑11 categories), facilitating translation into screening tools, scoring algorithms, and therapeutic case formulation.
