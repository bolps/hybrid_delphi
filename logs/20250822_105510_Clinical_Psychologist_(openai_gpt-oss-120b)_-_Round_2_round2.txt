### Why the eight‑factor solution is preferable  

1. **Theoretical fidelity** – The dimensions map directly onto the most widely accepted models of intimate‑partner violence (Power & Control Wheel, Stark’s coercive‑control framework).  
2. **Empirical consistency** – The Delphi synthesis reflected convergence of ≥ 4 independent expert analyses for each of the eight factors, indicating stable cross‑loadings.  
3. **Clinical utility** – Each factor isolates a different treatment target (e.g., cognitive‑restructuring for emotional/verbal abuse, safety‑planning for coercive control, financial‑empowerment for economic abuse, trauma‑focused work for fear/hyper‑vigilance, attachment work for neglect).  
4. **Parsimony without heterogeneity** – By moving “fear” and “neglect” out of the large emotional‑abuse cluster we avoid mixing *behaviour* (what the partner does) with *experience* (how the victim feels), preserving internal consistency of each scale.
