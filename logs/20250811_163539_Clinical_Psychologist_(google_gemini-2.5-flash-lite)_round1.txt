### Dimension 1: Emotional and Psychological Abuse

This dimension encompasses behaviors that aim to undermine an individual's self-worth, autonomy, and emotional stability through verbal attacks, manipulation, and constant criticism.

*   **Items:**
    *   Mi dice che non valgo niente e resterei sola se non stessi con lui perché nessuno mi vorrebbe (He tells me I'm worthless and would be alone if I didn't stay with him because nobody would want me.)
    *   Mi sento giudicata rispetto alle cose che faccio e come organizzo la mia vita (I feel judged about the things I do and how I organize my life.)
    *   Mi dice che non valgo niente e resterei sola se non stessi con lui perché nessuno mi vorrebbe (He tells me I'm worthless and would be alone if I didn't stay with him because nobody would want me.)
    *   Mi fa sentire spesso sbagliata e mi dice che è colpa mia se sta male (He often makes me feel wrong and tells me it's my fault if he's not well.)
    *   Urla o mi umilia di fronte ad altre persone (He yells or humiliates me in front of other people.)
    *   Mi butta giù, mi insulta o mi critica (He puts me down, insults me, or criticizes me.)
    *   Non mi fa sentire bene quando parla di come mi prendo cura di me stessa e del mio aspetto fisico (He doesn't make me feel good when he talks about how I take care of myself and my physical appearance.)

*   **Rationale:** These items directly reflect verbal aggression and devaluation, which are core components of emotional abuse. The aim is to erode the individual's self-esteem and create a sense of dependence.

### Dimension 2: Control and Isolation

This dimension focuses on behaviors designed to restrict an individual's freedom, social connections, and personal choices, thereby increasing their reliance on the abuser and isolating them from support systems.

*   **Items:**
    *   Parla male delle mie amiche e dice che sono delle “poco di buono”. Mi chiede di non vederle (He speaks badly of my friends and calls them "no-gooders." He asks me not to see them.)
    *   Dice che sono impegnata in troppe cose e che lo trascuro, vorrebbe che passassimo la maggior parte del tempo insieme (He says I'm involved in too many things and neglect him; he wants us to spend most of our time together.)
    *   Non mi sento libera di vestirmi come voglio (I don't feel free to dress as I want.)
    *   Mi sento costantemente controllata, ho la sensazione che non si fidi di me e che voglia sapere sempre dove sono e co chi, me lo chiede continuamente (I feel constantly controlled; I have the feeling he doesn't trust me and always wants to know where I am and with whom, he asks me continuously.)
    *   Non vuole che io veda la mia famiglia, le mie amiche o i miei amici. Mi dice che è lui adesso la mia famiglia, che loro cercano di separarci (He doesn't want me to see my family, my female friends, or my male friends. He tells me he is my family now, that they are trying to separate us.)
    *   Sento che mi manda troppi messaggi o mi chiama spesso, a volte in modo insistente (I feel he sends me too many messages or calls me often, sometimes insistently.)
    *   Cerca di controllare ciò che faccio e con chi mi vedo (He tries to control what I do and with whom I see.)
    *   Mi accusa di flirtare o di tradirlo/a (He accuses me of flirting or cheating on him.)
    *   Diventa estremamente geloso/ o possessivo/a (He becomes extremely jealous or possessive.)
    *   Minacce di distruggere le mie cose (telefono, vestiti, computer, auto, ecc.) (Threats to destroy my things - phone, clothes, computer, car, etc.)
    *   I miei amici mi hanno fatto notare che non piace loro come si comporta con me (My friends have pointed out that they don't like how he behaves with me.)
    *   Le mie amiche o la mia famiglia sono preoccupate per me (My female friends or my family are worried about me.)

*   **Rationale:** These items highlight manipulative tactics aimed at isolating the individual from their social network and controlling their daily activities and choices. Jealousy and possessiveness are often presented as justifications for this control.

### Dimension 3: Intimidation and Coercion

This dimension encompasses overt threats and actions designed to instill fear and compel compliance through the use of aggression, manipulation of personal information, and threats of self-harm or harm to others.

*   **Items:**
    *   Rompe e getta oggetti per intimidirmi (He breaks and throws objects to intimidate me.)
    *   Minaccia di fare del male a me, ai miei amici o alla mia famiglia (He threatens to harm me, my friends, or my family.)
    *   Quando faccio qualcosa che a lui non va bene poi non mi parla per giorni (When I do something he doesn't like, he doesn't talk to me for days.)
    *   Mi dice che, se lo lasciassi, rivelerebbe ad altri dei miei segreti o delle cose molto personali che sa di me (He tells me that if I left him, he would reveal my secrets or very personal things he knows about me to others.)
    *   Ha mostrato mie immagini intime ad altre persone senza il mio consenso (He has shown my intimate images to other people without my consent.)
    *   Minaccia di mostrare ai miei amici o ai miei familiari mie immagini intime (He threatens to show my intimate images to my friends or family.)
    *   Minaccia di farsi del male a causa mia (He threatens to harm himself because of me.)

*   **Rationale:** These behaviors are characterized by the use of fear and coercion to maintain control. This includes threats of physical harm, reputational damage (revenge porn), and emotional blackmail (threats of self-harm). The silent treatment is also a form of psychological coercion.

### Dimension 4: Physical Aggression and Violence

This dimension directly addresses acts of physical harm and violence, indicating a severe escalation of abusive behavior.

*   **Items:**
    *   Mi afferra per i capelli, mi strattona, mi mette le mani al collo, tenta di soffocarmi, mi dà dei pugni, mi schiaffeggia, mi getta addosso degli oggetti o mi fa male in qualche modo (He grabs me by the hair, shakes me, puts his hands on my neck, tries to suffocate me, punches me, slaps me, throws objects at me, or harms me in some way.)

*   **Rationale:** This is a clear manifestation of physical violence, a dangerous and illegal form of abuse that causes direct physical harm and trauma.

### Dimension 5: Gaslighting and Reality Distortion

This dimension refers to manipulative tactics where the abuser denies or distorts reality to make the victim question their own memory, perception, and sanity.

*   **Items:**
    *   Dopo che mi ha aggredito (fisicamente o verbalmente) nega di averlo fatto e mi fa dubitare che sia realmente successo (After he has attacked me (physically or verbally), he denies having done it and makes me doubt that it really happened.)

*   **Rationale:** Gaslighting is a specific form of psychological abuse designed to destabilize the victim's sense of reality and self-trust, making them more vulnerable to further manipulation.

### Dimension 6: Demands for Control and Unrealistic Expectations (Possessiveness/Enmeshment)

This dimension captures behaviors related to an unhealthy need for constant validation, proof of loyalty, and an inability to tolerate the partner's independence or separate life.

*   **Items:**
    *   Pretende prove d'amore e dimostrazioni di fedeltà nei suoi confronti (He demands proof of love and demonstrations of loyalty towards him.)
    *   Mi chiede insistentemente di fare sesso e non accetta quando dico di no o che non mi va (He insistently asks me to have sex and doesn't accept when I say no or I don't feel like it.)

*   **Rationale:** These items reflect an unhealthy attachment style characterized by a sense of entitlement and a lack of respect for boundaries, particularly concerning sexual autonomy and emotional validation. This can be indicative of insecure attachment or personality traits that lead to controlling behaviors.

### Dimension 7: Affective Instability and Mood Swings

This dimension describes erratic and unpredictable shifts in mood and behavior, which can create an environment of constant tension and anxiety for the partner.

*   **Items:**
    *   Ha forti oscillazioni d'umore, il minuto prima si arrabbia e mi urla contro e quello successivo è dolce e si scusa (He has strong mood swings; one minute he's angry and yelling at me, and the next he's sweet and apologizes.)
    *   A volte sento disagio o non voglio fare qualcosa ma lo nascondo per paura che si arrabbi (Sometimes I feel uncomfortable or don't want to do something, but I hide it for fear he will get angry.)
    *   Ho paura di vederlo perché so che potrebbe avere comportamenti che mi possono fare stare male o sentire in imbarazzo (I'm afraid to see him because I know he might have behaviors that can make me feel bad or embarrassed.)

*   **Rationale:** Rapid and unpredictable mood shifts are a hallmark of certain personality disorders and can create a highly stressful and unsafe relational environment. The partner's fear and avoidance behaviors are direct consequences of this instability.

This dimension includes items that describe positive, supportive, and respectful interactions within a relationship, serving as a contrast to the abusive patterns described in other dimensions.

    *   Sento che possiamo confrontarci e anche litigare senza farci male (I feel we can discuss and even argue without hurting each other.)

*   **Rationale:** These items describe the characteristics of a healthy, secure, and mutually respectful relationship. They highlight autonomy, open communication, emotional safety, and mutual support, which are the antithesis of abusive dynamics.
